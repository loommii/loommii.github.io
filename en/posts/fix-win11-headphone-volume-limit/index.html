How to Fix Windows 11 Headphone Volume Being Limited to 35%

loommii included in Windows Tips Troubleshooting
 2026-01-25  155 words   One minute 
Contents
  • The Problem
  • The Solution

The Problem

In Windows 11, after plugging in speakers or headphones, you may find that the maximum volume is capped at 35%, and you can’t increase it further, resulting in very low sound.

This issue usually occurs because the Microsoft operating system enables a volume cap by default for hearing protection.

The Solution

To solve this problem, we need to manually disable this limit.

  1. Right-click the speaker icon in the bottom-right corner of the taskbar and select “Sound settings”.

  2. In the Sound settings interface, scroll down and click on “More sound settings”.

  3. In the pop-up “Sound” configuration window, select the “Playback” tab.

  4. Find your headphone or speaker device, right-click on it, and select “Properties”.

  5. In the device properties window, switch to the “Custom” tab.

  6. Uncheck the “Limited Output” option.

  7. Click “OK” to save the settings.

After completing these steps, the volume limit will be removed, and you can freely adjust the volume up to 100%.
